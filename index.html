Organized since 1996, to protect, preserve and promote Hiphop as a conscious strategy.
The Temple Of Hiphop, founded by KRS-One and a select team of
activists who work to preserve Hiphop and its citizenry.
One of those activists, Sir Malik One has now self-published a book.

Peace In Time Of War:
Human Rights, Ethics & Sentient Messages to the Hiphop Citizen

Dedicated to all Hiphop Citizens

For those who show interest in the application and advancement of the founding principles and lessons of the Hiphop Preservation movement.

A non-partisan, independent, critical thinking tool, this book was written for the serious Hiphop enthusiast.Using empirical and cross-cultural analysis, this work examines qualitative values and metrics for risk assessment, paramount to the role of the Hiphop citizen, in the world. This work champions Hiphop citizens, especially all the working class families and independent critical thinkers, and seeks to preserve them beyond consumerism, beyond entertainment and commercialism.
